History

2024

What's Next?


I've been loving Baton Rouge, getting more involved in community here with sand volleyball at Mangos and DnD at Little Wars. After leaving Ethos Group in March, I haven't let any of my skills lapse. Instead, I'm learning about new Dev Tools, UX Design, and Accessibility. Who knows, I could be your next Web Developer!

2023

Graduation!


I moved 400 miles to Baton Rouge after leaving Texas A&M with a degree in Computer Science Engineering and a Minor in Business, developing full time with Ethos Group. Why make the move? My wonderful partner of 3 years, Amanda, landed a sweet research position at LSU as an Ornithologist!

2022

Senior Year


To cap off my time at A&M, I spent 6 months with the College of Nursing creating a website to connect rural folks with healthcare and community resources. I pulled double duty as both developer and project manager, eventually earning the College a sizable continuing research grant. Training a semantic search model was tough, but the real challenge was learning all about medicine and social work.

2021

First "Real" Job


After serving, delivering pizzas, and working at an escape room, I was ecstatic to land an internship at Ethos Group. Here I learned about creating clean and maintanable code while developing financial tools for the auto industry. I liked it so much that I stuck around for another internship and some part time work during the school year. Also, I adopted my dog Ronan from Aggieland Humane Society!

2018

The Freshman 15


I rolled up to A&M with zero clue what I wanted to do in life. Then I started writing some basic programming Python projects in Engineering 102 and it was love at first sight. I also became heavily invested in ASSIST, a service org where I coordinated 60 volunteers and planned an entire carnival for Hope For Three, a non-profit from my homeotown that supports families who have children with autism.
